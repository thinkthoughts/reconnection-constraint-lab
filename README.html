reconnection-constraint-lab

Constraint-gated modeling of magnetic reconnection across solar scales. This repo frames Ellerman bombs, plasmoid chains, turbulent current sheets, and large solar flares as a unified 45° geometric selection process rather than disconnected mechanisms.

45° constraint magnetic reconnection plasmoid instability turbulence DKIST / ALMA solar scales

Core statement

Magnetic reconnection can be presented as constraint-gated topology change. Energy remains stored while magnetic structure stays below a geometric alignment threshold. Once that threshold is crossed, topology rearranges and magnetic energy is released into heating, motion, and radiation.

cos θ ≥ 1 / √(1² + 1²)

Within this framing:

  • stable field structure = sub-threshold alignment
  • current-sheet transition = threshold crossing
  • reconnection onset = selected release pathway

Paper concept

Magnetic Reconnection as a 45° Constraint-Gated Energy Release Across Solar Scales

The paper would argue that reconnection across solar scales is best understood as a scale-invariant selection process:

constraint → signal > noise → structured energy release

That lets the same explanation cover:

Regime Standard label 45° systems explanation
small scale Ellerman bombs local threshold crossings in stressed magnetic structure
intermediate scale plasmoid instability recursive refinement of current-sheet selection
large scale X-class flares global cascade after large-scale threshold crossing

In this structure, turbulence is not treated as mere disorder. It is a high-dimensional search over local configurations where misaligned states are rejected and reconnection-compatible states survive.

Notebook roadmap

01 · Field alignment threshold

Generate synthetic vector fields, compute local angular distributions, and show a sharp transition near the 45° gate.

02 · Current-sheet instability

Track thinning sheets, increasing shear, and threshold crossing as a geometric onset condition for reconnection.

03 · Plasmoid cascade

Build a recursive splitting model where plasmoids appear as local re-balancing nodes inside the current sheet.

04 · Turbulent sampling

Model perturbative search over configurations and show how selection preserves structured release instead of undifferentiated collapse.

05 · Scale invariance test

Compare small and large events to test whether the same threshold logic survives across scales.

Observation and instrument tie-in

This repo is meant to connect directly to the seminar emphasis on observations and simulations:

  • DKIST resolves fine-scale magnetic structure and current-sheet morphology.
  • ALMA constrains thermal and magnetic evolution in reconnection-heated regions.
  • r-MHD simulations provide the full dynamical field space; the 45° interpretation extracts the selected reconnection subspace.

That makes the project useful as both an explanatory paper and a computational lab notebook series.

Initial glossary

  • magnetic reconnection: topological rearrangement of magnetic field structure that converts stored magnetic energy into heat, motion, and radiation
  • current sheet: thin region of strong magnetic shear where reconnection tends to localize
  • plasmoid instability: fragmentation of an extended current sheet into magnetic islands / plasmoids
  • turbulence: multi-scale fluctuation field that rapidly samples possible local configurations
  • geometric selection: threshold-based survival of reconnection-compatible configurations
  • 45° constraint: cosine gate used here to mark the transition between stored alignment and released topology change
  • scale invariance: same explanatory structure appearing from small bombs to large flares
